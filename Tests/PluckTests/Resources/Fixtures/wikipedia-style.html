Cookie

A cookie is a baked or cooked snack or dessert that is typically small, flat, and sweet. It usually contains flour, sugar, egg, and some type of oil, fat, or butter. Cookies may include other ingredients such as raisins, oats, chocolate chips, or nuts.

In most English-speaking countries except for the United States, crunchy cookies are called biscuits. Many Canadians also use this term. Chewier biscuits are sometimes called cookies even in the United Kingdom.

History

Cookies appear to have their origins in 7th century AD Persia, shortly after the use of sugar became relatively common in the region. They spread to Europe through the Muslim conquest of Spain.

By the 14th century, they were common in all levels of society throughout Europe, from royal cuisine to street vendors. With global travel becoming widespread at that time, cookies made for a fitting travel companion, a modern equivalent to today's protein bar.

Modern variations

Modern cookies come in countless flavors and shapes. Common varieties include chocolate chip, oatmeal raisin, peanut butter, snickerdoodle, and sugar. Specialty cookies range from biscotti to macarons to thumbprints filled with jam.

Many cookies are sold pre-packaged in boxes or bags, while others are baked fresh in stores and bakeries. Cookies are often paired with milk, coffee, or tea, and they are a staple of children's lunchboxes worldwide.
